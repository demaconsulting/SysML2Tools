Title: Force Fields — Coarse Phase (Step 2)

Purpose:
  Show the five force types acting simultaneously on a single block (Block A) during
  Fruchterman-Reingold coarse placement. Each force is a distinct coloured arrow with
  a label naming the force and giving its formula.

Layout:
  Block A (central, highlighted blue) occupies the centre of the canvas.
  Block B (connected neighbour, plain blue) sits to the upper-right.
  Block C (too-close obstacle, plain blue) sits to the lower-right.
  A canvas-centroid marker (green cross) sits to the lower-left.
  A dense-channel stripe (amber shaded rectangle) runs vertically on the right edge
  of the canvas, between Block A's column and the next.

Force arrows (all originate from Block A centre):
  1. Affinity spring   — blue solid arrow, direction NE toward Block B.
                         Label: "affinity spring  r(ŵ) = L·(1.5 − ŵ)"
  2. Block repulsion   — red solid arrow, direction NW (away from Block C, lower-right).
                         Label: "block repulsion  k² / d²"
  3. Hierarchy gravity — purple solid arrow, direction S (straight down).
                         Label: "hierarchy gravity  κ_h · Δlevel · L / k"
  4. Canvas gravity    — green solid arrow, direction SW toward centroid marker.
                         Label: "canvas gravity  κ_c · d_centroid / k"
  5. Wire pressure     — amber dashed arrow, direction W (away from dense channel on right).
                         Label: "wire pressure (annealed)  × T(t) → 0"
                         Dashed to signal that this force decays to zero as the system cools.

Key message:
  All five forces act at once; their vector sum determines where Block A settles.
  Wire pressure and the affinity spring are deliberately opposed (spring pulls toward
  Block B while pressure pushes away from the crowded channel between them) — this
  tension is what produces correct spacing. As temperature T(t) drops the wire-pressure
  term anneals away, leaving only the conservative spring/repulsion/gravity field to
  find a stable minimum.
