Title: Directed Flow Mode — Hierarchy Gravity Produces Clear Layers

Purpose:
  Show how strong hierarchy gravity (κ_h = 1.0) produces a clear top-to-bottom layered
  layout from the same graph used in mode-free-2d. Contrast pair with mode-free-2d.svg.

Layout:
  The same nine blocks from mode-free-2d, but now arranged in three horizontal layers
  (top, middle, bottom) reflecting their distance from the root of the hierarchy.
  Faint horizontal band shading or dashed horizontal lines divide the layers.
  Layer labels on the left margin: "Layer 0", "Layer 1", "Layer 2".

  Layer 0 (top): one or two root/source blocks.
  Layer 1 (middle): three or four intermediate blocks.
  Layer 2 (bottom): three or four leaf/sink blocks.

  All forward edges point strictly downward (top to bottom), drawn as straight or
  gently curved vertical lines with downward arrowheads.

  One back-edge (loop-back): a dashed arc going from a Layer 2 block up around the
  right side of the diagram back to a Layer 1 block, labelled "back-edge (loop)".

  The columns within each layer are ordered to minimise crossing — blocks are
  horizontally spaced to prevent edge crossings.

  Annotation: "κ_h = 1.0 — near-hard flow axis bias".

Key message:
  With κ_h = 1.0 the hierarchy gravity dominates. Blocks are pinned to their
  topological layer, producing a clear execution-order reading direction. Back-edges
  arc outside the main column to avoid cutting across forward flow. This is correct
  for Action Flow View and State Transition View.
