Title: Highway Assignment — Before and After

Purpose:
  Side-by-side comparison showing the same six-block graph before and after Highway
  Assignment (Step H). Left panel shows tangled naive routing; right panel shows how
  committed highway corridors with merged trunks and bundled wires clean it up.

Topology (same in both panels):
  Block 1 (top-left) connects to Block 4 and Block 6 — Bundle A
  Block 4 (top-right) connects to Block 2 and Block 3 — Bundle B
  Block 2 connects to Block 3 — local wire (same column)
  Block 5 connects to Block 6 — local wire (same column)
  Block 5 connects to Block 2 — single crossing wire (not bundled)

Left panel — "Before: tangled routing":
  Six blocks in two columns (1,2,3 on left; 4,5,6 on right). Seven crossing edges:
  1→4 (horizontal), 1→6 (diagonal), 4→2 (diagonal crossing 1→6), 4→3 (steep diagonal),
  5→2 (horizontal crossing), 2→3 (short local vertical), 5→6 (short local vertical).
  Arrowheads on all edges. Visual impression: spaghetti tangle.

Right panel — "After: highway corridors":
  Same six blocks. A tall amber shaded vertical corridor between the two columns.
  Bundle A (blue): ONE merged thick trunk exits Block 1's right face; at the corridor
  left wall a comb fans into two parallel blue wires. 1→4 passes straight through;
  1→6 runs down the corridor right wall to Block 6's level. Both have arrowheads.
  Bundle B (purple): ONE merged thick trunk exits Block 4's left face; at the corridor
  right wall a comb drops a spine and fans into two purple wires. 4→2 runs through
  corridor to left wall, then stubs down-left to Block 2; 4→3 similarly to Block 3.
  5→2 (gray): single thin wire crossing straight through the corridor, no bundle.
  2→3 and 5→6: short local vertical wires outside the corridor, unchanged.
  Small "A" (blue) and "B" (purple) labels inside the corridor identify the bundles.

Key message:
  Highway assignment groups crossing connections into committed corridors. Each
  block face has at most ONE merged trunk per bundle, eliminating redundant port
  slots. Inside the corridor, peak concurrent occupancy — not total edge count —
  determines the reserved width. Local wires (2→3, 5→6) and single crossing wires
  (5→2) remain outside the bundles.
