Title: Highway Width = Peak Concurrent Occupancy, Not Total Edge Count

Purpose:
  Illustrate that the reserved width of a highway corridor is determined by the
  maximum number of wires simultaneously present at any cross-section of the trunk,
  NOT the total number of edges that use the highway. Uses a vertical trunk with
  fan-out to show wires tapping off at different heights.

Layout:
  A tall vertical corridor strip in the centre of the image (amber rectangle,
  running top to bottom). On the right side, ONE source block (Block A) at the top.
  On the left side, THREE destination blocks (Block B at top, Block C in middle,
  Block D at bottom).

  Block A (right, y≈55) sends 3 wires through the corridor trunk:
    - Wire A→B: taps off at Block B level (y≈90)
    - Wire A→C: taps off at Block C level (y≈170)
    - Wire A→D: taps off at Block D level (y≈250)

  The trunk runs VERTICALLY on the right wall of the corridor (descending from
  Block A's level downward). The trunk is drawn as a set of parallel vertical lines
  (one per active wire) whose count decreases as wires tap off:

  Section 1 (y=55 to y=90):   3 parallel vertical lines — width = 3G
  Section 2 (y=90 to y=170):  2 parallel vertical lines — width = 2G  (A→B tapped off)
  Section 3 (y=170 to y=250): 1 vertical line           — width = G   (A→C tapped off)

  Horizontal tap-off lines with arrowheads exit the trunk at each block level and
  cross the corridor to reach Block B, C, D on the left.

  Width annotations on the left of the corridor:
    Brace or dimension line labelled "3G" for Section 1
    Brace labelled "2G" for Section 2
    Brace labelled "G" for Section 3

  Key formula annotation below or beside the diagram:
    "reserved width = peak(active wires) × G + 2·margin"
    "NOT: total_edge_count × G + 2·margin"

  A counter annotation on the right side showing the running active-wire count:
    "active = 3" at the top section
    "active = 2" at the middle section (with a small arrow showing A→B tapping off)
    "active = 1" at the bottom section (with A→C tapping off)

Key message:
  A highway with 3 total edges may only need 1G of width in its lower stretch once
  two wires have already tapped off. The closed-form compression in Step 6 uses the
  peak cross-section, computed by a sweep-line over entry/exit events — O(k log k)
  per bundle.
