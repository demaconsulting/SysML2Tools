Title: Gravity Compression — Three Stages

Purpose:
  Three left-to-right frames showing the same four-block layout progressing from the
  oversized starting state to the final compact state. Illustrates Step 4 → Step 6
  (oversized placement → gravity compression).

Frame 1 — "Oversized (expansion_factor = 3×)":
  Four blocks arranged in a 2×2 pattern with very large gaps between them — both
  horizontal and vertical gaps are roughly three times larger than needed. The
  routes connecting them are clean and uncongested (minimum-bend straight lines)
  because there is ample space. The large gaps are emphasised by double-headed arrows
  labelling the gap sizes "3× min_gap". Visual impression: clean but very wasteful.

Frame 2 — "Compressing":
  Same four blocks; gaps are now roughly at 1.5× the minimum. Bold inward arrows on
  all four sides of the layout show the compression direction (both axes simultaneously).
  The connector routes are still clean. The gaps are visibly smaller than Frame 1 but
  the layout is not yet at minimum.

Frame 3 — "Minimal (closed-form)":
  Same four blocks; gaps are now exactly at the minimum feasible width — just enough
  for the committed wires and labels. Double-headed arrows label the gap sizes with
  the formula "max(min_gap, N×G + 2·margin)". The connector routes remain clean
  (no detours forced by compression). Blocks are noticeably closer together than
  Frame 1. Visual impression: compact and tidy.

Between frames: right-pointing arrows labelled "compress" to show progression.

Key message:
  Starting oversized guarantees routing is trivially clean in Step 5. Compression then
  shrinks gaps to their exact minimum in a single closed-form pass (no iterative loop).
  The result is minimum-size without ever having produced a congested intermediate.
