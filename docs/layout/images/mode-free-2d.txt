Title: Free 2D Mode — Clusters Emerge from Force-Directed Relaxation

Purpose:
  Show how isotropic force-directed placement (κ_h = 0.15) produces natural 2D
  clusters without any pre-assigned rows or columns. Contrast with mode-directed-flow
  which imposes a top-to-bottom layer structure.

Layout:
  Approximately nine blocks total, forming two visible spatial clusters.

  Cluster 1 (left area, four blocks): A tightly connected group — a 2×2 arrangement
  where all four blocks have high mutual affinity (many shared edges). The blocks sit
  close together. Short edges between them. A light background ellipse or rounded
  rectangle loosely encircles the group, labelled "community 1".

  Cluster 2 (right area, three blocks): A triangle of connected blocks. Again a faint
  background region labelled "community 2".

  Two satellite/peripheral blocks: connected to only one cluster each; they nestle
  against the nearest free face of their cluster rather than floating far away.

  Edges between clusters: one or two longer edges crossing the gap between the two
  communities, noticeably longer than the intra-cluster edges.

  No grid lines, no row/column rules — positions look "organic" and physics-driven.
  A small annotation: "rows / columns emergent — not pre-assigned".

Key message:
  With κ_h = 0.15 the hierarchy gravity is weak. Blocks are free to cluster in 2D;
  the natural result is community groupings matching the model's package/type
  relationships. This is the correct mode for General View where there is no single
  execution order.
